Startups Are Pushing AI Into Patient Care

By Brian Gormley
WSJ PRO   March 23, 2026 6:00 am ET
…RecovryAI, has created virtual care assistants designed initially to assist patients after joint replacements. The company expects these assistants will one day be prescribed by doctors to help patients stay on track with their recovery, according to co-founder and Chief Executive Scott Walchek. The company, which is testing the technology in clinical trials, is seeking regulatory authorization through the FDA, he said.
“The regulatory hurdle is difficult and it’s going to be difficult for anyone to compete against,” Walchek said.
