Intro

RecovryAI is made up of two parts — a patient-facing app, and a provider dashboard. They work together in real time.
03
The App
14–26s
Let's start with the patient experience. Following surgery, the patient meets their physician-prescribed virtual care assistant. It delivers clinically validated guidance, tailored to the patient and their procedure. And it's available 24/7.
04
Check-In
26–40s
Each day, the assistant prompts the patient to check in on key recovery factors. Here is an example of a slider for level of pain. It follows up with other recovery factors, including sleep, medication, diet, and activity.
05
Score
40–46s
These check-ins feed into a single recovery score, a shared view of progress for the patient and provider.
06
Response Types
46–52s
Based on the recovery data, the assistant responds in one of three ways. A normal response, a provider escalation, or an emergency escalation.
07
Normal Response
52–60s
First, let's take a look at the normal response. The assistant provides personalized guidance, answering questions and offering reassurance.
08
Escalation
60–70s
Next, a provider escalation. The assistant notices something unusual, follows up directly. The patient confirms the pain has been getting worse, and the assistant flags it for the care team.
09
Emergency
70–80s
And finally, an emergency escalation. A patient reaches out with chest pain. The assistant recognizes the severity, advises calling 911, and notifies the provider immediately.
10
Transition
80–84s
→
Now let's look at what the care team sees.
11
Portal
84–96s
On the provider dashboard, the care team gets a complete view of each patient. Recovery score, history, clinical context, conversations, notes, and recommendations.
12
Clinic
96–110s
Zooming out, providers can see every patient at a glance. ER recommendations, provider connect, resolved events, and messages — all organized.
